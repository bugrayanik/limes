0 · THE GAME — v3 CORE

The Living Frontier

"The border is the battle." One shared 8 × 8 board. Eight painted Stake tokens — one per column — form the frontier line itself. Push troops past a stake with support behind them and the border walks a row forward; the land behind your line is where your farms live. Advancing stakes trample enemy fields (raid for loot, or annex them); every unit fighting beyond your own border pays +1 Crop and loses its guard bonus — geometry taxes the leader, no charity mechanics. Win by sacking all three enemy Supply Wagons, or by Standard Rout: surround the enemy hero and their army breaks — a visible checkmate. Full concept: design/v3-concept.md.

No Net-Decks

Six interlocking locks: tactics cards that pass to your opponent when played (Onitama), seeded face-up setups, max 2 new units/round (armies grow in plain sight), a shared finite unit supply that taxes the meta comp, a one-copy doctrine market with aging prices, and Root-style tribe verbs — 64 asymmetric matchups.

No Snowball

The frontier is the rubber band: the player pushed back fights compact, supported and cheap while the leader's spearhead pays upkeep strain. Lost ground banks Tribute chips spent on mid-clash interventions. Dead units are wounded, not dead — only Wagons die forever.

No Dead Time

Clashes are a fixed two pulses (~40 s) of countable hits, pushes and charges, with face-down tactic flips and loser-funded interventions inside them. Damage = Atk + bonuses − Guard, every modifier exactly ±1 — chess-countable in your head. No initiative coin flip: phases resolve simultaneously.

The round (~70–90 s, 12–15 rounds/match): Muster (harvest behind your line · upkeep · build fields/doctrines · recruit ≤2 face-down + reposition ≤2) → Reveal (screens lift together) → Clash (2 pulses: secret tactic flip → telegraphed Brace / Ranged / Move / Melee resolution → tribute interventions) → Frontier (stakes step, fields burn or change flags, wagons take breach damage) → Pass (your played tactic slides to the opponent). Caravan drafts r4/r8 favor whoever trails; from r12 the land itself exhausts so turtling becomes starvation; golden-goal rounds force an ending. Economy: just Supply + Crop — the 4-resource matrix is retired.

⚖ Balance verification in progress — a simulation pipeline (Python resolver, policy bots, ~20,000+ matches) is testing the acceptance contract: round-1 winner converts 55–65%, lead changes after r7 in 25–35% of games, turtle/aggro within 45–55%, sandbagging EV-negative. Scorecard lands in this section when it completes.

I · STYLE DNA

The Three Pillars

Painted Miniature, Not Toy

Every character is a premium tabletop miniature: realistic proportions, intricate armor engraving, hand-painted weathering. Chibi and plastic-toy looks are banned. The fantasy is a collector's wargame table come alive.

Color Is Identity

One signature hue per tribe — readable from across the board before any silhouette is parsed. Eight fully distinct hues, each paired with one metal accent. No two tribes share a color family.

The Engine Draws the Rules

AI paints art; Godot draws information. Grids, card frames, stats, costs and text are engine-rendered — always crisp, always exact (3×3 village, 8×6 front), never hallucinated.
